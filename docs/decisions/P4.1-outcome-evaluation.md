MarketScalper never executes trades (v1.2). "Outcome" here is a
HYPOTHETICAL, candle-based evaluation ‚Äî "what would this recommendation

## A16.1 ‚Äî Lifecycle states (P4.2)

A recommendation row is born `active` (D21.2). It transitions exactly
once, to one terminal state, on the FIRST triggering condition per bar
(deterministic order):

1. **invalidated** ‚Äî any of (¬ß7): (a) the strategy's INVALID rule fires
   ‚Äî the entry zone is not filled within `invalid_after_bars` closed
   candles after creation (5, from the Signal); (b) an opposite-direction
   signal of the same strategy family fires for the symbol while this
   recommendation is still `active`; (c) a G1 data-integrity fail on the
   symbol (feed gap / clock loss) while `active`. `status_reason` records
   which.
2. **expired** ‚Äî the thesis has not resolved within the evaluation
   horizon (A16.3) and none of the above fired.
3. **evaluated** ‚Äî the hypothetical position reached a terminal outcome
   (SL or a TP). The evaluator (A16.2) writes `eval_*` and the lifecycle
   sets `status='evaluated'`.

`active ‚Üí {invalidated | expired | evaluated}` are all TERMINAL; a
terminal row never transitions again (the append-only-core discipline ‚Äî
only the status/eval columns mutate, via the P0.7 `update_recommendation_*`
helpers). Lifecycle runs per closed 1m candle over the symbol's own
stream (replay ‚â° live).

## A16.2 ‚Äî Hypothetical outcome evaluator (P4.3)

Pure function of the recommendation's `entry/sl/tp1/tp2/direction` and the
closed 1m candles from the creation bar onward. No execution, no fills ‚Äî
a candle-geometry simulation.

**Entry trigger.** The position "enters" when, within the
`invalid_after_bars` window, a candle's range reaches the entry price
(`low ‚â§ entry ‚â§ high`), or a candle GAPS through it (LONG: `open ‚â§ entry`;
SHORT: `open ‚â• entry`) ‚Äî a limit-style fill. Fill price = `entry` on a
range touch, or the candle `open` on a gap-through (the realistic worse
fill). Entry never triggered in the window ‚Üí `eval_outcome='none'`,
`eval_r=NULL`, and the lifecycle marks the row `invalidated` (INVALID
rule (a)) ‚Äî an untriggered setup has no outcome.

**Terminal resolution** (from the fill bar onward, up to the horizon):
first touch of `SL` or `TP1` ends the position (the 1R decision point;
¬ß7's "TP1 par 50% off" is management guidance, but the single-outcome
evaluator resolves at TP1 ‚Äî conservative, and TP2 reachability is
recorded in MFE).
- **SL-first ambiguity** (¬ß7, verbatim): if one candle's range spans BOTH
  SL and TP1, the outcome is `sl` (worst case).
- **Gap-through at open** (¬ß7): if a candle OPENS beyond a level (LONG:
  `open ‚â§ SL` or `open ‚â• TP1`; SHORT mirror), that level is resolved at
  the candle `open` (SL: a loss worse than 1R; TP1: a gain at the gap
  price), evaluated before the same bar's range touches ‚Äî a gap-open
  beyond SL is `sl` even if the bar later prints TP1.
- Level-touch order within a non-gapping bar: SL checked before TP1
  (the ambiguity rule), so exact-touch of both = `sl`.

**Outputs** (schema columns, all numeric R multiples signed for the trade
direction):
- `eval_outcome` ‚àà `{'sl','tp1','tp2','none'}`. `'tp2'` is recorded only
  when a gap-through skips TP1 and opens at/through TP2 on the resolving
  bar (TP1 is between entry and TP2, so a normal monotone move always
  hits TP1 first; `'tp2'` is the gap-skip case). Otherwise `'sl'`/`'tp1'`/
  `'none'` (horizon reached un-resolved after a valid entry ‚Äî the trade
  neither stopped nor targeted; `eval_r` = mark-to-market at the horizon
  bar's close).
- `eval_r` = realized R = `(exit ‚àí fill)/(fill ‚àí SL)` for LONG, mirrored
  for SHORT (`(fill ‚àí exit)/(SL ‚àí fill)`). The R normalizer is the risk
  taken AT the fill (`fill ‚àí SL`), which equals the planned risk
  (`entry ‚àí SL`) on a range-touch fill but differs on a gap-through fill
  (`fill ‚âÝ entry`) ‚Äî the realized-R semantics, not the planned-R. Fees
  are NOT re-applied here (the D17 `net_rr_tp1` already carries the
  fee-adjusted plan RR; `eval_r` is the raw candle-geometry outcome ‚Äî the
  analytics layer (P4.11) compares the two). `NULL` only for un-triggered
  (`'none'` pre-entry). **Degenerate-fill guard:** if the entry gaps
  through the stop (`fill ‚â§ SL` for LONG, `fill_risk ‚â§ 0`), the position
  is an immediate catastrophic stop ‚Äî outcome `'sl'`, `eval_r` measured
  against the (positive) planned risk that the gap already exceeded
  (`(fill ‚àí entry)/(entry ‚àí SL)`, a loss worse than ‚àí1R), resolved on the
  fill bar itself.
- `eval_mae` = max ADVERSE excursion over the held window, in R
  (LONG: `(min(low) ‚àí fill)/(fill ‚àí SL)` ‚â§ 0). `eval_mfe` = max FAVORABLE
  excursion, in R (LONG: `(max(high) ‚àí fill)/(fill ‚àí SL)` ‚â• 0) ‚Äî captures
  whether TP2 (or beyond) was reachable. Both over fill‚Üíexit bars
  inclusive.

## A16.3 ‚Äî Evaluation horizon

`EVAL_HORIZON_BARS = 240` closed 1m candles after the FILL bar (4 hours;
uncalibrated, P5-owned ‚Äî the D9 doctrine). Reaching the horizon with no
SL/TP1 touch ‚Üí `eval_outcome='none'` with `eval_r` = mark-to-market at the
horizon bar close; the lifecycle marks `expired` if the horizon is hit
before any terminal touch. (Distinct from ¬ß7's 15-minute management "time
stop", which is display-only guidance; the evaluator needs a longer,
explicit deterministic bound so slow setups still record an outcome.)
The entry window (`invalid_after_bars = 5`) and the post-fill horizon are
independent: an un-filled setup invalidates at 5 bars; a filled one runs
to a terminal touch or 240 bars.

## A16.4 ‚Äî Determinism

Pure candle fold; no clock, no randomness; the same recommendation +
candle stream yields byte-identical `eval_*` in replay and live
forward-run (the P4.4 tests pin this alongside the SL+TP-same-candle,
gap-through, and expiry-before-entry edge cases; the P4 gate's forward
run cross-checks replay). No engine is touched ‚Äî the evaluator and the
lifecycle are NEW composition-level modules consuming frozen outputs +
the recommendation rows.

## A17 ‚Äî Snapshot fallback (P4.6 auto-context)

¬ß8's journal auto-context wants a chart PNG at recommendation time (an
LWC `takeScreenshot` ‚Üí server save). A17 pins that the backend NEVER
depends on a browser screenshot: the AUTHORITATIVE auto-context is the
`state_snapshot` JSON already persisted on the `signals` row (D21.1) ‚Äî
the complete structure payload, sufficient to reconstruct the ¬ß8 card and
the full rule-trace deterministically. The chart PNG
(`journal.chart_snapshot_path`) is a best-effort UI enhancement, captured
only when a browser session is present (the live UI, P4.6/P4.7), and is
NULL in headless forward-runs and replay. Journal analytics and the
system-vs-actual comparison (P4.11) read the state_snapshot + rule-trace,
never requiring the PNG. `journal.reason_text` is seeded at P4.6 from the
signal's facts + the qualification reasons already in the state_snapshot
(no recomputation ‚Äî the ¬ß8 trace is pure projection).

  only (v1.2). "Fill" is a hypothetical price, never an order.

  R multiple, equity-independent.
